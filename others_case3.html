Teaching Point:

  • Giant liver hemangiomas, defined as hepatic lesions greater than 5 cm, are considered atypical due to their size and potential complications. Though rare, they can spontaneously rupture or be ruptured by trauma, with a mortality rate of 36-39%.
  • Another serious but less common complication is Kasabach-Merritt syndrome, a form of disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC) that requires prompt intervention.
  • Diagnosing giant hemangiomas can be difficult due to their similarity to other hypervascular tumors, such as hepatocellular adenomas, hepatocellular carcinomas, and metastases from neuroendocrine or renal cell carcinomas.
  • Diagnosis usually starts with abdominal ultrasonography, followed by CT or MRI for confirmation and further evaluation.
  • Technetium-99m-labeled red blood cell (Tc-99m RBC) scintigraphy is a highly specific, non-invasive imaging method for detecting hepatic hemangiomas.
  • This technique shows a characteristic mismatch between perfusion and blood pool, with initial decreased perfusion in early dynamic phases, followed by increased radiotracer uptake during the blood pool phase.
  • Initially, the lesion appears "cold" or less active, but it becomes more intense with significant radiotracer accumulation 1‚Äì2 hours after injection.
  • The sensitivity of Tc-99m RBC scintigraphy depends on lesion size: 17‚Äì20% for lesions smaller than 1 cm, 65‚Äì80% for lesions between 1 and 2 cm, and nearly 100% for those larger than 2 cm.
  • When combined with Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT), the specificity remains 100%, regardless of lesion size, making it an effective diagnostic tool.
  • Despite its high diagnostic accuracy, Tc-99m RBC scintigraphy is no longer the primary method for diagnosing hepatic hemangiomas.
  • This shift is due to various limitations, including limited availability, high costs, prolonged procedure times, radiation exposure, and the development of more advanced imaging technologies that offer improved capabilities for hepatic imaging.
