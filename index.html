About the Website

Welcome to the Division of Nuclear Medicine and Molecular Imaging Teaching File Repository.
This website was developed using teaching cases contributed by our division to support education in Nuclear Medicine and Molecular Imaging. We also encourage collaborative case sharing with divisions and departments across the country in order to foster a comprehensive educational resource and promote academic exchange within the medical imaging community.
Each section of the repository is dedicated to a specific imaging modality or study type. Cases are structured to encourage an image-first approach to interpretation, allowing learners to independently review imaging findings before accessing the diagnosis, key teaching points, and final impressions. Through this format, the repository aims to support trainees, technologists, residents, fellows, and practicing physicians in developing systematic image interpretation skills while broadening their exposure to a diverse range of clinical cases.

Who We Are

The Division of Nuclear Medicine and Molecular Imaging at University of Ottawa is part of the Department of Medicine and operates across the General, Civic, and Riverside campuses of The Ottawa Hospital.
Our division is composed of staff physicians, clinical fellows, residents, physicists, graduate students, technologists, and administrative personnel dedicated to clinical excellence, education, and research in Nuclear Medicine and Molecular Imaging.
The University of Ottawa Nuclear Medicine Residency Program transitioned to the Competence by Design (CBD) curriculum in 2020, reflecting our ongoing commitment to excellence in postgraduate medical education and competency-based training.

Goals and Objectives

  • Provide case-based learning covering core nuclear medicine studies.
  • Reinforce interpretation skills through examples with explanations.
  • Highlight common, rare and high-yield pathologies encountered in clinical practice.
  • Integrate relevant anatomy, physiology, and radiopharmaceutical principles.
  • Develop systematic approaches to image interpretation.
  • Encourage differential diagnosis formulation based on imaging patterns.
  • Correlate nuclear imaging findings with clinical presentation and other modalities.

How to Use These Cases

  • Review relevant information about the case (ex: Age, symptoms, clinical question etc).
  • Review the images.
  • Formulate a differential diagnosis.
  • Compare your interpretation with the provided findings and impression.
