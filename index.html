About This Resource

Welcome to the Department of Nuclear Medicine and Molecular Imaging's Teaching File Repertory!
This website was created thanks to cases from our department, and potentially in conjunction with departments throughout the country.
Each section focuses on a specific imaging modality or study type, with cases designed to encourage image-first interpretation prior to revealing key findings and impressions.

Who Are We?

The Nuclear Medicine and Molecular Imaging Division at the Ottawa Hospital covers 3 sites: General, Civic and Riverside.
The division is made up of multiple staff, residents, physicists, technologists and administrators.
The University of Ottawa Nuclear Medicine Residency Program now offers a Competence by Design program since its transition in 2020.

Goals and Objectives

  • Provide case-based learning covering core nuclear medicine studies.
  • Reinforce interpretation skills through examples with explanations.
  • Highlight common, rare and high-yield pathologies encountered in clinical practice.
  • Integrate relevant anatomy, physiology, and radiopharmaceutical principles.
  • Develop systematic approaches to image interpretation.
  • Encourage differential diagnosis formulation based on imaging patterns.
  • Correlate nuclear imaging findings with clinical presentation and other modalities.

How to Use These Cases

  • Review relevant information about the case (ex: Age, symptoms, clinical question etc.)
  • Review the images
  • Formulate a differential diagnosis
  • Compare your interpretation with the provided findings and impression
